EventKey

An app that helps users to find, purchase, and store tickets for events that they love.

Tools Used

Project Overview

EventKey is a ticketing app aimed to help people who often miss out on events and value transparency in pricing to learn about and purchase tickets for upcoming events.

The Problem

There are several ticketing services and virtual event newsletters that are currently on the market. Currently none of these options are incredibly efficient at notifying users about upcoming events in time for them to learn about and register for these events, nor do they give clear and open pricing, which is causing users to miss out on events that they would be interested in attending, and if they are able to find an event they would like to attend, they feel they are overpaying for tickets. We believe that if we design an app that is able to more efficiently notify users of upcoming events in their area  and allow them to register for said events with clear and open pricing, our users will miss out on fewer events that they are interested in attending and feel more confident that their tickets are priced fairly.

Project Goals

To create an application that helps users to discover upcoming events specific to their interests.
Give users transparency into the pricing of tickets.
Allow users to easily find and store their tickets in-app.

Research

To better understand our user, I started conducting user interviews with friends and colleagues who often attended and purchased tickets for local events.

During these research phase, I also tried to understand:

  • Why participants attend events, who do they bring with them, when they attend events, and what kind of events they attend.
  • Participant's thoughts and previous experiences with event registration/ticketing services.
  • Learn what tools participants use when searching for events to attend, and how the feel about those tools.

User Persona

Problems Faced by Users

A lack of transparency in ticketing prices on competitor sites, most of the time they do not see the “true price” of tickets until they reach check-out.
Only hearing about events after they have already happened.
Find that many ticketing sites do not give nearly enough information about the actual event such as location, what the event is about, or activities at the event.

Possible Solutions

Based on the all the information collected through research and findings, we determined that an application would require following functionality to address our users painpoints.

Provide users with recommendations for upcoming events tailored to their interests.
Have users select interest when signing up and give them the ability to edit/change their interests at any time.
Provide the “real” price for a ticket before reaching checkout.
Send notifications to users about upcoming events.
Allow users to purchase and manage their tickets in-app.
Send reminders for events that the user has purchased tickets for.
Provide information that users desire in-app so no additional research about events are needed.

Key Takeaways

In working with this team we were able to discover problems that our users faced in their event searching and ticketing process and design a solution that addresses those problems.

We did this by researching our target users and competitors, designing wireframes and clickable prototypes, and testing and iterating on those prototypes to deliver an enjoyable user experience.

Given more time we would want to implement more features and functionality and continually test and iterate upon our design.
